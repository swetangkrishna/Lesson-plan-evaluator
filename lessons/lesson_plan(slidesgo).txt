Your lesson plan
Introduction to AI Literacy and Its Use Cases. Objective: By the end of this lesson, students will be able to define AI literacy, identify various use cases of AI across different industries, and critically analyze the implications of these use cases

Lesson Overview
Topic: Introduction to AI Literacy and Its Use Cases Target Audience: University Students
Duration: 90 minutes
Delivery Method: In-person classroom session

Learning Objectives
By the end of this lesson, students will be able to:
1. Define AI literacy and its importance in the modern world
2. Identify and explain AI use cases across different industries
3. Critically analyze the ethical and practical implications of AI technologies 4. Understand the potential impact of AI on future professional landscapes

Materials Needed
1. Laptop or computer with projection capabilities 2. Presentation slides
3. Whiteboard and markers
4. Handouts with AI use case scenarios
5. Group discussion worksheets
6. Optional: Short video clips demonstrating AI applications

Lesson Structure
I. Introduction (15 minutes):
- Define AI literacy
- Brief overview of AI's current landscape
- Importance of understanding AI in modern education and careers
II. AI Use Cases Exploration (30 minutes):
- Healthcare: Medical diagnostics and personalized treatment
- Finance: Fraud detection and algorithmic trading
- Education: Personalized learning and adaptive assessment
- Technology: Natural language processing and computer vision - Business: Customer service chatbots and predictive analytics
III. Interactive Group Activity (25 minutes):
- Divide students into small groups
- Assign different industry scenarios
- Groups analyze AI applications, benefits, and potential challenges - Prepare a 5-minute presentation on their findings
IV. Ethical Implications Discussion (15 minutes): - Privacy concerns
- Bias in AI systems
- Job market transformations
- Responsible AI development


Assessment Strategies
1. Group Presentation Evaluation - Depth of industry understanding - Critical analysis of AI use cases - Clarity of communication
2. Individual Reflection Assignment
- 500-word essay on personal perspectives of AI literacy - Due within one week of the lesson
3. Participation and Engagement
- Quality of contributions during group discussions - Thoughtful questions and insights shared

Additional Resources
1. Recommended Readings:
- 'AI Superpowers' by Kai-Fu Lee
- MIT Technology Review articles on AI
2. Online Platforms:
- Coursera AI literacy courses
- Google AI education resources - OpenAI learning materials
3. Suggested Follow-up Activities:
- Attend AI ethics workshop
- Participate in AI project simulation - Join university AI research groups

Adaptability Notes
Hybrid/Online Adaptation:
- Use breakout rooms for group discussions
- Utilize collaborative online whiteboards
- Record key segments for asynchronous learning
Differentiation Strategies:
- Provide additional support materials for students with less technical background - Offer advanced research challenges for more tech-savvy students
